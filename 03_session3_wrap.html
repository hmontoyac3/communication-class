Communication · Session 3 · The Synthesis
WHAT YOU NOW KNOW.
Takeaway 1

The Message Is Not the Words

7% words. 38% voice. 55% body. You communicate constantly — even when you say nothing. The Brief exercise proved it: the same message, filtered through different minds, came out completely different. This is your operating reality in every company.

Takeaway 2

Structure Creates Trust

The best Shark Tank pitches today weren't the most polished — they were the most structured. Hook → Problem → Solution → Proof → Ask. When someone is confused, they say no. When they're clear, they say yes. Clarity is a competitive advantage.

Takeaway 3

Warm First, Then Competent

Cuddy was right. The pitches that got imaginary investment were the ones where you trusted the person before you believed their numbers. In every important conversation, your first job is to be believed as a human — not as a slide deck.

The path forward: You've now been in Teams (how groups work and fail), Communication (how ideas travel — and distort). Next comes Negotiation — where both skills collide in real time under pressure. Everything you practiced today matters there. How you communicate an offer, how you read the other person, how you control your presence when the stakes are real — it all starts with what you learned today.
